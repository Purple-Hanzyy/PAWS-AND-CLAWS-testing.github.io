Tips

  1. In order to reduce the risk of infections, clean the ears of your pets, brush their teeth and bath with pet-safe shampoo.
  2. Make sure fresh water is always available.
  3. Create a safe space for your pets, a resting area with appropriate beddings and toys.
  4. Play with them regularly so that they can maintain a healthy weight as well as preventing boredom.
  5. Make sure they are well vaccinated and protected against ticks, fleas and worms.
  6. Their coat needs to be brushed regularly so as to reduce shedding and provide healthy skin.
  7. Train them by using treats and praises so that they have a good behavior.
  8. As pets need lots of love and attention, spend quality time with them.
  9. Learn more about their body language to better meet their needs.
  10. Do not leave your pet alone for long period of times.
  11. Make sure your pets are tagged with an identification collar, to easily identify them.
  12. Provide your pet with regular check-ups to the veterinary.

How to Cater a Dog

  1. Pet food need to be kept cool in a sealed container, dry place that is less than 30 degrees Celcius.
  2. Warm the food before serving your pet, as they have a keen sense of smell, it can smell more appetizing.
  3. Feed your dog as least twice a day.
  4. Avoid leftover food after eating, as human food is richer in calories and fats, your pets may gain weight.
  5. Regular check-ups to the veterinary are very important as well as vaccination that need to be done.
  6. Walk your dog daily, so as to reduce anxiety and maintain the physical health of your pet.
  7. Make sure that your dog will be able to socialize with people and other animal.
  8. Keep toxic plants and foods away such as chocolate, grapes and lilies.
  9. Keep them mentally strong by stimulating it by puzzles and toys.
  10. If your pet can go outside freely, it is recommended to have a fenced area where the dog can play and run

How to Cater a Cat

  1. Feed your cat food that is appropriate for their age, weight, and level of activity.
  2. Place a litter box in a safe, quiet environment that is easily accessible and make sure it is cleaned daily.
  3. Keep small objects they might ingest away.
  4. Provide a scratching post or trim your cat's nails to maintain their claws.
  5. Regularly brush your cat to minimize shedding and prevent hairballs.
  6. Install climbing structures like a climbing tree or shelves to satisfy their natural instincts.
  7. Check regularly for signs of fleas, ticks, or irritations in their fur and skin.
  8. Pay attention to changes in appetite or litter box habits, as they could indicate health issues.
  9. Respect their independence and provide a quiet space for them to retreat.
  10. Ensure windows and balconies are securely closed to prevent accidents.
